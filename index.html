The Story of D-CoE: Summary of Previous Broadcasts

The D-CoE at IISc is dedicated to connecting academic research with real-world industry needs. Through various broadcasts, they've highlighted their progress in promoting design and innovation, showcasing advanced training methods, and building upon IISc's history of design education. This particular broadcast focuses on the new, state-of-the-art Industry 4.0 labs that will be available for the department to develop cutting-edge products and conduct research using the latest industry technologies. The broadcast will also detail the courses offered by D-CoE.

D-CoE's Cutting-Edge Facilities for DM, Startups & MSMEs

Discover 10 state-of-the-art facilities designed for startups, MSMEs, and students that the department can leverage to transform ideas into reality.

Design and Innovation Labs

  • Industry 4.0 Lab: Develop collaborative robots and autonomous systems with AR/VR tools.
  • Mechanical Prototyping: Create precision designs with advanced CNC machines.
  • Electronic Prototyping: Build IoT devices, robots, and FPGA-based systems.
  • Design Thinking Spaces: Explore visualization and ideation areas tailored for innovation.

Support Infrastructure

  • Virtual Training Spaces: Enhance learning with immersive online sessions.
  • Content Creation Studio: Craft high-quality multimedia content.
  • Business Development Center: Drive entrepreneurial success with dedicated support.
  • Ergonomics Lab: Focus on human factors for better designs and usability.

D-CoE's Tailored Programs for Every Stakeholder that DM can leverage

D-CoE courses give department students access to resources, expert trainers, and industry professionals. This can improve their learning and may lead to career opportunities through MOUs and project collaborations. These interactions and increased departmental visibility create a better learning environment and expose students to new ideas.

Empowered MSMEs

DM can leverage cutting-edge facilities like CNC machining and Metal Additive Manufacturing to integrate smart manufacturing into its processes. This allows CPDM to create complex prototypes, produce customized parts, and explore innovative designs. Learn cost-effective strategies to improve operational efficiency and competitiveness.

Learning from Skilled Professionals

Direct engagement with skilled professionals offers DM several key advantages:

  • Enhanced Skills: Acquiring practical skills and expertise.
  • Industry Insights: Gaining up-to-date knowledge of Industry trends.
  • Networking Opportunities: Building valuable connections with Industry professionals.

Learning from Industry Practices

CPDM can gain knowledge by examining how other organizations utilize customized training, Industry 4.0 labs, and operational strategies in their work.


Course Structure

The structured curriculum covers essential aspects of design and prototyping. Each module is carefully tailored to meet industry standards, fostering skills that are directly applicable to modern design and engineering challenges.

Design Modules

  • Design Thinking Methodology
  • Aesthetics and Semantics
  • Ergonomics and Human Factors
  • Business Development

Prototyping Modules

  • Design Prototyping
  • Mechanical Prototyping
  • Electronic Prototyping
  • Industry 4.0 Technologies
